SALES

Places

  • Calle Crisologo, Vigan

    The most famous and major tourist magnet in Vigan, Calle Crisologo is full of Spanish-styled houses
    that gives people of this generation a glimpse of the Spanish era. This street also features the prosperous lifestyle
    of the said era because of the Manila-Acapulco trade.

  • Island Souvinir, Vigan

    It is the only town during World War II that was saved from destruction because of a love story.
    Legend has it that a Japanese General, who married a Filipina in Vigan, promised the parish priest
    that he would save the town from destruction from the retreating Japanese if he would agree to take care of his family.

  • Hotel Luna, Vigan

    The first and only museum hotel in the Philippines managed by Artstream Hospitality Management Group Inc.
    It is conveniently located at the at the heart of the Heritage Village in Vigan City,
    which is just a block away from Crisologo street.

  • Cafe Leona, Vigan

    Its eccentricity lies in their serving of a fusion of Filipino, Japanese, and Italian cuisine, therefore catering to both expats and locals with their extensive menu

  • Irene's Empanada, Vigan

    (If you are coming from Cafe Leona, walk along Calle Crisologo and turn left at Calle Salcedo. You should see it on your left after the corner of Calle V. de los Reyes with Gordion Hotel as the landmark and after Insiang's empanadahan.)
